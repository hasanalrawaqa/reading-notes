# data structure discussion questions
###  How can I apply deliberate practice to my own learning and skill development?
 Deliberate practice involves breaking down complex skills into smaller components and practicing them in a focused and structured way. To apply deliberate practice to our own learning and skill development, we can start by identifying specific areas we want to improve and breaking them down into smaller, achievable goals. We can then practice these skills in a structured way, seeking feedback and adjusting our approach as needed.
    
###  How can I identify and overcome my own biases and blind spots? 
Identifying and overcoming biases and blind spots requires self-awareness and a willingness to seek out feedback from others. We can start by reflecting on our own assumptions and beliefs, and examining how they might be influencing our decisions and behaviors. We can also seek out diverse perspectives and feedback from others to help us identify areas where we might have blind spots or biases.
    
### How can I cultivate grit and resilience in the face of challenges and setbacks?
 Cultivating grit and resilience involves developing a growth mindset and a sense of purpose, and practicing deliberate practice. We can also learn from our failures and setbacks, reframing them as opportunities for learning and growth.
    
### How can I define my own vision of success and measure my progress towards it? 
Defining our own vision of success involves identifying our values and passions, and setting meaningful goals that align with them. We can then track our progress towards these goals, seeking feedback and adjusting our approach as needed.
